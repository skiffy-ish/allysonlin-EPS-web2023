A Short Bite of the History of Halloween!

By Allyson Lin

The spoOoOky season has arrived! Halloween is getting closer every day, so here are some fun facts about the (long) history of Halloween’s modern day traditions,
including why we eat so much candy and dress up on Halloween!

Have you ever wondered why you dress up in scary costumes for Halloween? Well, this modern-day tradition
may have its roots in an ancient pagan festival originating in Ireland and Scotland called Samhain. On
Samhain, the Celtic people would light bonfires and wear scary costumes, as they believed that at the end
of the harvest season and winter’s beginning, the barrier between the world of the living and the dead
was at its weakest, and therefore some assorted evil spirits would roam the Earth until the barrier
thickened up again. In an effort to scare off these assorted supernaturals, the Celts would wear costumes
on October 31, a day marked part of Samhain.

But, what about candy? Why, exactly, is this delicious (and unhealthy) snack so popular around Halloween?
We already talked about Samhain and how the Celts tried to scare off assorted evil spirits during it, but
that’s not the only thing the Celtic people did in this three night long celebration. Samhain was also a
time to honor the souls of the dead, and the Celts people did that by leaving out various kinds of treats
on their doorstep. Eventually, after Samhain received large changes due to the invasion of Ireland and
Scotland by the Catholics in the 1st century, Samhain went through multiple evolutions over a large number
of years, going from Samhain to All Hallows Eve, All Saints Day and All Souls Day, and then finally evolving
into the Halloween we know and love today.
